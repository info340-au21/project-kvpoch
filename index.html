Welcome to UW Library Directory!

Problem Description

Our project will encompass the needs of those who seek more information about UW Libraries. What handicap accessibilities are available in each of the libraries? Where can I find more information on what the noise levels are like inside different sections of the libraries?
These are some questions that we would like to help bring more light on and to help those in need of these types of resources, as they are currently hardly available and rarely detailed. Some resources that are already available to attempt to answer these questions is the University of Washington’s Library Website . As well as some more specific resources on each of the libraries and their respective details of the library. These resources are already available but are hard to navigate and to use. Although the website includes as much information as possible, at first glance it is still hard to find all the information that a student would need: noise levels, available capacity, handicap accessibility, technology availability. Our goal is to ease access to these types of information, by collecting and listing all this information based on students’ needs.

Proposed Application

The users of the application will be students on the University of Washington Seattle Campus. The users will be able to:

  • View information about different on-campus libraries
  • Filter and sort out different features on preferences
  • View noise levels, outdoor seating, cafe and food options, handicapservices, technology availability, and more
  • Leave reviews and ratings on their favarite libraries

This application will help solve our chosen problem because it will give more guidance to individuals who have trouble finding the best library for them to study in. Often students will choose the most popular libraries, Odegard and Suzzalo, but can never find a proper table to sit in because it’s often crowded. This application will broaden their horizons to exploring different libraries that can suit their needs.
